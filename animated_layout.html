What is this page for?

This page has two purposes. One is to act as a method to teach myself static website design by playing around with CSS/Javascript/HTML designs.

The other purpose is to build a portal to my programming projects which can act to summarize them by using the designs I learn from my dedication to improving myself in this field.

Why go through this trouble?

I have been learning programming practices in Java, C#, Python, Javascript, HTML/CSS, PHP, SQL, and XML for the last three years in order to one day obtain employment as a software developer. I think that it's getting close to the time where I believe in my own skills enough that I can get serious about creating a presence for myself while applying for positions.

I believe that building this website will help give a human element to the applications I send, and I hope that someone sees this and can tell just how hard I am trying to get my foot in the door of this exciting field.

Why Programming?

Initially I wasn't sure what I wanted to do when I set out to community college at the age of 27, almost 6 years ago now.

I tried accounting, and while the math wasn't hard, the backwards thinking of debits and credits was confusing for my mind, and after two semesters I gave that up to try another major.

For the next year I tried out graphic design, thinking that maybe that could be an enjoyable career, but I found that I wasn't a good artist!

Well, I'm not one to fall for the sunk cost fallacy, so I decided to switch gears to programming. I thought, "Hey, I like video games, it would be cool at the very least to be able to make them as a hobby." And I am extremely grateful to my past self for making that decision because I realized how much I loved programming and the challenges it brings.

There is a certain rush involved when I am stuck on a difficult bug for a long time and finally fix it. I also like learning new things, and with programming, there is no end to new things to learn. It's also one of the first skills I ever obtained through hard work and my own determination. I am proud of what I have learned and that also fuels me to keep on going. I hope to become masterful in many areas of programming as time goes on.

What kind of employment?

I would love a career in video gaming, but as I've learned more I realize that I'm a better programmer than I am a video game developer. Of course, I won't give that up as a choice, and would be open to any interested parties looking for game developers. Also, it feels like getting an interview with a video game company has become akin to winning the lottery!

About Me

About five years ago I decided that I was tired of following a certain path of hardships brought on by my own lack of motivation. I realized that I was living a life where I assumed that things would just eventually get better while simultaneously shooting myself in the foot by waiting for some magical motivation to come.

Instead of waiting, I decided to make my own motivation by forcing myself to learn new things, and eventually I landed on programming as the ultimate career choice. I used to want to be a photographer, and a graphic design artist, or anything really that could pay the bills beyond an entry level job. I spent a couple years developing those talents before I realized that I did not enjoy doing them.

But it's different for programming. I can feel it in my blood that I will never stop learning new tricks, techniques, and tactics for making my software development skills stronger.

I have a huge passion for solving challenging problems, and I also love video games, hoodies, and food!
